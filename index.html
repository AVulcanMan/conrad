The Problem

Weight Constraints

Lithium battery packs with cooling systems and casings can weigh 6–8 kg per kWh, limiting eVTOL payload and range.

Safety Risks

Lithium-ion batteries use volatile electrolytes that can release flammable gases when overheated or damaged, creating fire hazards.

Cost & Sustainability

Lithium and cobalt are expensive and environmentally taxing. At scale (100k units/year), costs can reach $153/kWh on a usable-energy basis.

Our Solution: NimbusPower

NimbusPower is a rechargeable sodium-ion pouch battery designed specifically for eVTOLs, featuring an integrated passive thermal cooling coating.

✓ Sodium-Ion Chemistry

Sodium is 1000x more prevalent in Earth's crust and 60,000x more prevalent in oceans than lithium, improving cost and scalability.

✓ Pouch Architecture

Pouch cells reduce wasted space and weight compared to cylindrical cells, optimizing packaging for aerospace applications.

✓ Passive Thermal Coating

Integrated high-emissivity composites and phase-changing materials mitigate overheating and thermal runaway without heavy active cooling systems.

Technology & Innovation

NimbusPower combines aviation-specific architecture, safety-first chemistry, and optimized thermal management in a single integrated system.

Component Specifications
  • Anode: Hard carbon (high capacity, stable cycling)
  • Cathode: Prussian Blue Analog / Layered NaMO2 (high voltage, good capacity)
  • Electrolyte: NaPF6 in carbonate solvents (stable, non-flammable)
  • Separator: Microporous polymer (prevents short circuits)
  • Coating: High-emissivity composites + thermally conductive polymers + phase-changing materials
Novelty & Protectability
  • Aviation-specific architecture: Distributed pouch cells optimized for eVTOL airframe integration
  • Safety-first chemistry: Sodium-ion reduces fire risk vs lithium-ion
  • Optimized thermal coating: Replaces heavier active cooling systems
  • IP Protection: Utility patents (coating + architecture), trade secrets (coating composition), design patents (aerospace configurations)

Risks & Mitigations

Lower Energy Density vs Li-ion

Risk: Sodium-ion batteries have lower energy density, potentially limiting range or requiring larger packs.

Mitigation: Optimize pouch architecture for weight efficiency; distribute cells throughout airframe for better weight distribution; target applications where cost and safety outweigh density tradeoff.

Certification Timeline & Safety Validation

Risk: Aviation certification is lengthy and rigorous; safety validation requires extensive testing.

Mitigation: Engage early with FAA/EASA; partner with certified test facilities; leverage sodium-ion's inherent safety advantages; conduct comprehensive testing program.

Thermal Coating Material Validation & Durability

Risk: Coating materials must withstand aviation conditions and maintain performance over time.

Mitigation: Accelerated aging tests; environmental testing (temperature, humidity, vibration); iterate on material composition; validate with real-world cycling tests.

Supply Chain & Manufacturing Scale-up

Risk: Scaling production requires reliable supply chains and manufacturing partners.

Mitigation: Establish relationships with sodium-ion cell manufacturers early; develop multiple supplier options; license manufacturing to established partners; leverage sodium's abundance for supply security.
